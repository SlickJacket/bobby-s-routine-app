## Design Context

### Users
Bobby BuffaloBoy — single user, software engineer at CoolHealth (day job, funding the real goal), working toward COO by Dec 2026, but his actual ambition is to leave software and work full-time as a writer/director/showrunner (current project: Pickupline). Uses the app one-handed on his phone, likely several times a day — a morning check-in, habit toggles through the day, quick idea capture in the moment something occurs to him, a weekly review of Insights. Stated problem is consistency, not motivation — friction kills the habit.

### Brand Personality
Theatrical/marquee identity — Bobby is a performer, the app should feel like backstage/showbiz, not generic productivity software. Voice: plain, active, no apology in errors, no fake enthusiasm ("Saved" not "Yay, saved!"). Light theatrical framing in labels is fine ("Cues" for habits, "Marquee" as the daily-log screen name, "Encore" as the app name) but never at the cost of clarity — a first-time user should understand every control without translation. Avoid names that read as literal film/TV production jargon (e.g. "Call Sheet" was rejected specifically for this reason) — theatrical framing should evoke the feeling of performance, not name-drop specific industry documents.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark theme only, always. Existing palette: near-black bg (`#14151f`), panel surfaces (`#1c1e2c` / `#232538`), gold accent (`#f2c14e`) as the primary/marquee-bulb color, velvet (`#c0355e`) as secondary accent, teal (`#5eead4`) for streaks/positive states, warm ivory ink (`#f5f1e8`) text, muted slate (`#8b8fa3`) secondary text. Bebas Neue display font (condensed, marquee-poster feel) + Inter body. Signature device: a row of "marquee bulbs" that light up left-to-right as daily progress is made.

### Design Principles
1. Fewer taps beats more features — every visual enhancement must not add friction or steps.
2. Cold open under 1s on a mid-range phone — no bundle-bloating effects, nothing that blocks the main thread.
3. Celebrate completion, don't guilt failure — motion and color should reinforce wins (bulbs, streaks), never shame missed days.
4. Backstage/showbiz, not corporate productivity software — theatrical framing (marquee lights, spotlight, curtain-like textures) is welcome; sterile SaaS-dashboard polish is not.
5. Plain, confident voice carries into visuals too — no fake enthusiasm, no gratuitous decoration for its own sake.
